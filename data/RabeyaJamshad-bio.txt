Rabeya Jamshad is a Ph.D. candidate in the Healthcare Robotics Lab at UC San Diego and is advised by Dr. Laurel Riek. Her work focuses on developing robots that can support human teams in dynamic, fast-paced, and uncertain environments. It lies at the intersection of human-robot interaction, reinforcement learning and team science research. In her work, she explores how we can predict human intentions and team dynamics to determine appropriate robot actions. She has also designed experiments in collaboration with cognitive science researchers to support the understanding and design of robots in non-dyadic teams. Rabeya has worked with stakeholders such as clinicians and emergency response team members to design robots that can support their tasks. Before joining UC San Diego, Rabeya completed her M.Sc. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Georgia Institute of Technology as a Fulbright Scholar where she was advised by Dr. Ayanna Howard. She earned her B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from LUMS, Pakistan. She is also a recipient of the Powell Fellowship 2021 at UC San Diego.
